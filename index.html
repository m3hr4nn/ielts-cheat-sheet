Writing Task 1

20 minutes150+ words

Structure (4 Paragraphs - NO Conclusion)

  1. Introduction - Paraphrase the question (20-30 words)
  2. Overview - Main trends, NO specific data (30-40 words) ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT
  3. Body 1 - Detailed analysis with data (50-70 words)
  4. Body 2 - More detailed analysis with data (50-70 words)

Essential Collocations

Function Key Phrases
Increases experience/witness a dramatic rise, soar to, climb steadily, reach a peak of
Decreases show a marked decline, plummet to, drop steadily, reach a low of
Stability remain stable, level off at, show minimal variation, fluctuate slightly
Comparisons significantly higher/lower than, roughly equal to, twice as much as
Overview starters Overall, it is clear that..., The most striking feature is..., What stands out is...
Proportions account for X%, constitute a significant proportion, dominate the market
Time references at the beginning of the period, by the end, throughout the timeframe, from X onwards

Critical Rules

✅ Overview is MANDATORY (worth 25% of score)

✅ NO conclusion paragraph

✅ Use past tense for completed periods, present for current data

❌ Never repeat the introduction at the end

❌ No personal opinions or speculation

Writing Task 2

40 minutes250+ words

Structure (4 Paragraphs)

  1. Introduction - Paraphrase + Thesis statement (40-50 words)
  2. Body 1 - Main argument with P.E.E.L. (80-100 words)
  3. Body 2 - Second argument with P.E.E.L. (80-100 words)
  4. Conclusion - Summarize + restate position (40-50 words)

Four Essay Types

1. Opinion (Agree/Disagree)

  • Clear position in introduction
  • 2 body paragraphs supporting your view
  • Restate position in conclusion

2. Discussion (Both Views)

  • ¶2: First viewpoint
  • ¶3: Second viewpoint + your opinion
  • Show which side you support

3. Advantages/Disadvantages

  • ¶2: Advantages (2-3 points)
  • ¶3: Disadvantages (2-3 points)
  • State which outweighs (if asked)

4. Problem-Solution

  • ¶2: Problems (2 problems with explanations)
  • ¶3: Solutions (2 solutions with expected outcomes)

P.E.E.L. Body Paragraph Structure

  • Point - Topic sentence
  • Explain - Develop the idea
  • Example - Specific evidence
  • Link - Connect back to thesis

Essential Collocations

Function Phrases
Arguments compelling argument, widely accepted, hotly debated issue, strongly advocate
Trends undergo dramatic changes, witness a sharp increase, gain widespread popularity
Cause/Effect directly contribute to, stem from, have far-reaching implications, trigger concern
Solutions tackle the problem, implement effective measures, viable solution, sustainable approach
Advantages substantial benefits, outweigh the drawbacks, reap the benefits
Academic tone empirical evidence suggests, convincingly demonstrate, merit serious consideration

Linking Words

Purpose Words
Adding Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally, What is more
Contrasting However, Nevertheless, On the other hand, Conversely
Examples For instance, To illustrate, A case in point is
Cause Due to, Owing to, As a result of
Effect Consequently, Therefore, Thus, Hence
Emphasizing Indeed, Undoubtedly, Particularly, Especially

Critical Rules

✅ Clear position throughout

✅ Well-developed arguments with examples

✅ 250+ words (aim for 270-280)

✅ Academic tone (no contractions, slang)

❌ No personal examples ("I went to Paris...")

❌ No memorized essays

❌ Must address ALL parts of question

Speaking Part 1

4-5 minutes

Structure

  • Introduction (30 seconds)
  • 3 topic sets with 3-4 questions each

D.E.E. Answer Formula (15-25 seconds)

  1. Direct answer (1 sentence)
  2. Extend with details/reasons (1-2 sentences)
  3. Example or extra thought (1-2 sentences)
Example

Q: "Do you enjoy cooking?"

"Yes, I really enjoy cooking, especially on weekends when I have more time. I find it quite therapeutic and it allows me to experiment with different Persian and international dishes. Just last week, I tried making homemade pasta for the first time, and it turned out surprisingly well."

Essential Phrases

Purpose Expressions
Likes I'm really into, passionate about, quite keen on, fond of
Dislikes not particularly fond of, not really a fan of, not my cup of tea
Frequency on a daily basis, every now and then, once in a blue moon, as often as I can
Reasons The main reason is..., This is partly because..., One reason why...
Changes It's changed dramatically, evolved considerably, not as... as it used to be

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ One-word answers

❌ Memorized speeches

❌ Over-thinking (respond naturally)

❌ Going off-topic

Speaking Part 2

3-4 minutes

Timing

  • 1 minute - Preparation (write keywords only)
  • 2 minutes - Speaking (monologue)
  • 30 seconds - Rounding off questions

Speech Structure

Section Time Content
Introduction 10-15 sec Brief opening
Bullet 1 20-25 sec First point with details
Bullet 2 20-25 sec Second point with details
Bullet 3 20-25 sec Third point with details
Explain part 30-40 sec Final bullet (why/how/feelings)
Conclusion 10-15 sec Brief summary (optional)

Preparation Notes Example

Topic: Describe a skill you'd like to learn

• What: Python/AI programming
• How: Online courses, practice, Claude
• Why: Career development, personal interest
• Useful: Better DevOps, automation, jobs
Examples: ML apps, automate tasks

Opening & Transition Phrases

Opening Phrases

  • "I'd like to talk about..."
  • "The person/place I'm going to describe is..."
  • "I'm going to tell you about a time when..."

Transition Phrases

  • Moving on to...
  • As for...
  • Regarding...
  • When it comes to...
  • Another thing worth mentioning is...

Extension Techniques

  1. Describe in detail - "absolutely captivating, with bustling markets..."
  2. Add feelings - "What I particularly appreciate is..."
  3. Give specific examples - "For instance, I learned how to..."
  4. Make comparisons - "Unlike traditional methods..."
  5. Discuss past/present/future - "Initially... now... in the future..."

Essential Collocations

Category Phrases
People warm-hearted, down-to-earth, remarkably patient, close bond
Places bustling atmosphere, breathtaking views, conveniently located, lasting impression
Events eye-opening experience, memorable occasion, thoroughly enjoyed, changed my perspective
Skills steep learning curve, constant practice, highly beneficial, master the fundamentals

Critical Rules

✅ Address ALL bullet points

✅ Speak for full 2 minutes

✅ Use specific examples and details

❌ Don't write full sentences in notes

❌ Don't memorize speeches

❌ Don't stop at 1 minute

Speaking Part 3

4-5 minutes

Nature

  • Abstract, analytical discussion
  • 5-7 questions
  • 40-60 seconds per answer
  • Tests critical thinking

I.R.E.E.C. Answer Structure

Element Description
I - Initial response Direct answer (I believe..., In my view...)
R - Reason 1 First supporting argument
E - Example Specific evidence or example
E - Extend Additional perspective/reason 2
C - Concession/Conclusion Acknowledge other views or conclude

Question Types

1. Opinion

"Do you think technology has made life easier?"

Structure: Opinion + Reason 1 + Reason 2 + Contrasting view + Overall

2. Comparison

"How has education changed?"

Structure: Past situation + Present + Reasons + Impact

3. Future/Prediction

"How will AI affect jobs?"

Structure: Current trend + Future possibility 1 + Possibility 2 + Uncertainty

4. Problem-Solution

"What can reduce traffic congestion?"

Structure: Problem + Government solution + Individual solution + Challenges

Essential Vocabulary

Purpose Phrases
Opinions I'm firmly convinced, I tend to think, It seems to me, On balance
Causes The primary cause is, This stems from, Can be attributed to, A combination of factors
Comparisons In contrast to, Considerably more, Whereas X focuses on, By contrast
Trends Growing trend toward, Increasingly prevalent, Gradually disappearing, Undergone transformation
Adding points Furthermore, What's more, Beyond that, In addition
Contrasting On the other hand, Having said that, Conversely, Then again
Acknowledging Admittedly, I should acknowledge, To be fair, Granted
Future In all likelihood, It's quite likely, It's conceivable, It remains to be seen

Advanced Grammar for Band 8+

Conditionals:

  • "If governments invested more, we'd see reduced inequality."
  • "If technology hadn't advanced, we might not face these challenges."

Passive Voice:

  • "This can be attributed to several factors..."
  • "These policies have been implemented successfully..."

Relative Clauses:

  • "People who grow up bilingual, which is increasingly common, often demonstrate enhanced flexibility."

Cleft Sentences:

  • "What concerns me most is the growing inequality..."
  • "It's the lack of political will that prevents change."

Handling Difficult Questions

Buy time:

  • "That's an interesting question. Let me think..."
  • "That's quite a complex issue..."
  • "That's thought-provoking..."

Acknowledge complexity:

  • "This is a multifaceted issue with no simple answer..."
  • "There are various perspectives on this..."

Use hedging:

  • Tend to, generally, typically, often, probably
  • Many, some, certain
  • To some extent, to a degree

Common Mistakes

❌ Personal examples only → Use societal analysis

❌ Short answers (15 sec) → Aim for 40-60 seconds

❌ Agreeing with everything → Show balanced views

❌ Too casual → Use academic vocabulary

❌ "I think" repeatedly → Vary expressions

English Tenses Master Guide

Essential grammar reference for IELTS Writing & Speaking

Simple Present

Structure

Subject + base verb (+ s/es for 3rd person singular)

Time Reference

Habitual/Repeated Actions & Universal Truths

⟲ NOW ⟲

Example

"She works at a hospital every weekday."

Present Continuous

Structure

Subject + am/is/are + present participle

Time Reference

Actions Happening at This Moment

← NOW →

Example

"I am preparing for my IELTS exam this month."

Present Perfect

Structure

Subject + have/has + past participle

Time Reference

Past Action Connected to Present (Unspecified Time)

PAST ═══▶ NOW

Example

"I have studied English for five years."

Present Perfect Continuous

Structure

Subject + have/has been + present participle

Time Reference

Ongoing Action with Duration (Past → Present)

PAST ≈≈≈▶ NOW

Example

"They have been living in London since 2020."

Simple Past

Structure

Subject + past verb (regular: base + ed, or irregular form)

Time Reference

Completed Action at Specific Past Time

● PAST | now

Example

"I took the IELTS mock exam last year."

Past Continuous

Structure

Subject + was/were + present participle

Time Reference

Action in Progress at a Past Moment

PAST ← ● → | now

Example

"I was studying when she called me yesterday."

Past Perfect

Structure

Subject + had + past participle

Time Reference

Earlier Past Action Before Another Past Action

● EARLIER → ● PAST | now

Example

"I had finished my homework before dinner started."

Past Perfect Continuous

Structure

Subject + had been + present participle

Time Reference

Duration of Action Before Past Moment

EARLIER ≈≈≈▶ ● PAST | now

Example

"She had been working there for two years before she quit."

Simple Future (will)

Structure

Subject + will + base verb

Time Reference

Predictions, Spontaneous Decisions, Promises

now | ● FUTURE

Example

"I will take the IELTS exam on November 20th."

Simple Future (going to)

Structure

Subject + am/is/are going to + base verb

Time Reference

Planned Actions, Intentions, Clear Evidence

now | ● FUTURE

Example

"I am going to study grammar tonight."

Future Continuous

Structure

Subject + will be + present participle

Time Reference

Action in Progress at Future Time

now | ← ● → FUTURE

Example

"This time next week, I will be taking my IELTS exam."

Future Perfect

Structure

Subject + will have + past participle

Time Reference

Action Completed Before Future Point

now ═══▶ ● FUTURE

Example

"By December, I will have completed my IELTS exam."

Structure

Subject + will have been + present participle

Time Reference

Duration Until Future Point

now ≈≈≈▶ ● FUTURE

Grammar Terms Explained

Base Verb (Infinitive without "to")

The simplest form of a verb, without any tense or conjugation.

Examples: work, study, go, eat, write

Past Participle

The verb form used with perfect tenses and passive voice. For regular verbs, add -ed. Irregular verbs have unique forms.

Regular: worked, studied, played

Irregular: gone, written, taken, eaten, been

Present Participle

The verb form ending in -ing, used in continuous tenses.

Examples: working, studying, going, eating, writing

Gerund

A verb ending in -ing that functions as a noun. While identical in form to the present participle, gerunds name activities or states.

Examples: "Swimming is healthy." / "I enjoy reading."

Note: In "I am swimming," swimming is a present participle. In "Swimming is fun," swimming is a gerund.

Auxiliary Verbs (Helping Verbs)

Verbs that help form tenses, questions, and negatives.

Common auxiliaries: am, is, are, was, were, have, has, had, will, would, shall

Final Tips

Writing

  • Plan before writing (5 minutes Task 2)
  • Always include overview in Task 1
  • Use collocations naturally (8-12 per essay)
  • Check for errors (last 3-5 minutes)
  • Never copy word-for-word from question

Speaking

  • Extend every answer (never one sentence)
  • Use specific examples
  • Show range of grammar and vocabulary
  • Stay calm and natural
  • Part 3: Think like a university seminar

Practice

  • Record yourself speaking (check timing)
  • Write timed essays weekly
  • Learn collocations in context
  • Get feedback on your work
  • Review band descriptors regularly
